LOOK BACK


A Short Film by Lauren Beck



Synopsis

Stella had the gift of premonition. Her sister, a performer known as the Mimic, has the uncanny ability to translate images into sound. Though separated by death, they are bound together by a glass eye and the man to whom it has been entrusted. Look Back asks what it means to return to a place that has been lost. The story examines the unexpected possibilities of what can happen when that reckoning proves impossible.

Cast & Crew

Lauren Beck—a filmmaker and visual artist based in Austin, Texas—wrote, directed and produced Look Back. Lauren has taught visual art at the University of Chicago and worked with young filmmakers on Chicago's South Side. Her films explore cosmology and ritual through experiments with narrative discontinuity. Lauren has exhibited her drawing and video work both nationally and internationally.

Look Back is based on a short story by Teddy Jefferson, from his collection One Inch Leather: 14 Stories. Teddy is the author of numerous stories, essays, and plays, including The Wedding, The Desk, and The Insomniac. His translation of Pirandello's However You Want Me (Come tu mi vuoi) won the PEN translation prize.

Josh Duensing served as Director of Photography and Editor on Look Back; you can check out his work here. Josh is currently at work on a film adaptation of The Willows, a novella by Algernon Blackwood.

Look Back stars Colin Reeves as the Man and Cindy Henkin as the Mimic. Colin is a Chicago based actor who has recently appeared in Body Awareness (Dandelion Theater), Kokola (Akvavit Theater Company) and What Was Mine to Give (Strangeloop Theater). Cindy, also Chicago-based, premiered her solo work, Betwixt Between, at the Chicago Fringe Fest; she also appeared in The Club for Modern Fashions, Josiah McElheny's installation at The Arts Club of Chicago.

Noted composers Kris Davis, Benoît Delbecq, and Cenk Ergün all contributed to the score of Look Back.
